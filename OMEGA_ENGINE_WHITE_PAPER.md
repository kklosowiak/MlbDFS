## 🎛️ Part 1: How to Read the Dashboards

The OMEGA Cockpit is divided into five dedicated panels. Each panel has been built from the ground up to give you instant, color-coded visual clarity. Here is how each column works and how you should read them.

### 1. The Starting Pitchers Matrix
This is your pitching anchor terminal. It helps you identify high-floor "Core" pitchers to build around and high-leverage "Pivots" to separate your lineups from the crowd.

*   **OMEGA (Final Score):** The definitive quantitative score of the pitcher (on a scale of 0 to 110+). A score of **100+ is elite (blue)**, **85+ is high-quality (green)**, and anything below is average or low-tier.
*   **PHY (Physics Score):** Represents the pitcher's raw physical talent and stuff. It translates their **SIERA** (Skill-Interactive ERA) and **CSW%** (Called Strikes + Whiffs) into a simple 0-25 score. **Higher is better.** A high Physics score means they are pitching incredibly well physically, regardless of their team's record.
*   **MKT (Market Score):** The betting market's confidence in this pitcher. It tracks line movement, odds shifts, and sharp betting volume. A **high Market score (purple)** means the smartest money in the world is heavily backing this pitcher.
*   **ALPHA SIGNALS:** This column displays raw positive indicators like `🦈 SHARK` or `🐳 WHALE` (explained in Part 2).
*   **ALPHA CONTEXT:** Displays negative warnings or environmental conditions like `🚨 TRAP`, `⚠️ PARADOX`, or wind direction.
*   **PITCHER & vs OPPONENT:** The starting pitcher's name and matchup. Clicking any column header allows you to instantly sort the slate from best to worst.
*   **K-PROP & OUTS:** The live betting lines for their Strikeouts and total Outs. The parenthesis show the odds (e.g., `+114` means a $100 bet wins $114). Use these columns to quickly find which pitchers are projected for deep, high-strikeout outings.

### 2. The Hitters Core Power Rankings
This matrix ranks individual batters, exposing elite players who are in premier matchups to hit home runs or accumulate extra-base hits.

*   **OMEGA (Hitter Score):** The raw projected power potential of the batter on today's slate. Ranks individual hitters from highest power upside to lowest.
*   **ALPHA SIGNALS & CONTEXT:** Flags whether the hitter is an active `🎯 TARGET` or faces key environmental factors.
*   **PLAYER & vs PITCHER:** The batter's name, team, and the opposing starting pitcher they are facing.
*   **HR-PROP & BASES:** The live Vegas prop pricing for their Home Run odds (e.g., `+450` means a $100 bet pays $450 if they hit a home run) and total bases line. Sorting by this column lets you instantly identify the highest-upside power hitters on the slate.

---

### 3. The Team Stack Sentiment Rankings
In large-field DFS tournaments, "stacking" (pairing 3 to 5 hitters from the same team) is the most successful strategy to win first place. This dashboard ranks the best offensive team stacks on the slate.

*   **OMEGA (Stack Score):** The overall rating of the team's offense today. A score above **95 is an elite GPP stack**.
*   **PHY (Physics Score):** The raw statistical matchup rating. Evaluates the team's aggregate batting statistics against the opposing starting pitcher's specific weaknesses.
*   **MKT (Market Score):** Tracks sharp money flows backing this team to score runs.
*   **ITT (Implied Team Total):** The most important basic stat in DFS. This is the **Vegas Implied Run Total** (e.g., `5.28` means Vegas expects this team to score 5.28 runs). Stacking teams with an ITT above **4.8** is a premier strategy.
*   **ML MOVE & TT MOVE (Moneyline & Team Total Movement):** Shows how betting lines have shifted since opening. For Team Totals (TT), a positive move (e.g., `+0.5`) is positive steam. For Moneyline (ML) in American odds, a negative shift (e.g., `-15`) indicates the price became more expensive/favored, meaning heavy money is flooding in to back them (styled green as 'val-up' in your cockpit).
*   **DIVERGENCE:** Shows the "Money Gap." If this is **highly positive (e.g., +26%)**, it means smart, high-roller bettors are putting far more money on this team than the general public. This is a massive buy signal.

### 4. The Platoon Splits Advantage Matrix
Baseball is a game of matchups. Left-handed batters have a physical advantage against right-handed pitchers, and vice versa. This matrix cross-references these splits to find hidden edges.

*   **VS HAND OPS & wOBA:** Shows how well this team hits against the specific hand of today's opposing starting pitcher (LHP or RHP). An **OPS above .730** indicates a highly lethal matchup.
*   **OPS DIFF (Differential):** Represents the "platoon jump." A positive score (e.g., `+62`) means this team hits today's pitching hand significantly better than the opposite hand.
*   **PITCHER VS LHH/RHH OPS:** Shows the opposing starting pitcher's vulnerability. If a pitcher has a high OPS allowed vs Right-Handed Hitters (e.g., `.768`), you should aggressively target the right-handed batters on the opposing team!
*   **ADVANTAGE:** Instant color-coded badges indicating the strength of the matchup:
    *   `⚡ ELITE`: A massive statistical mismatch (OPS > .780).
    *   `🎯 STRONG`: A highly favorable matchup (OPS > .730).
    *   `🚨 TRAP`: A severe platoon disadvantage—fade this stack!

---

### 5. The Ballpark Weather & Environmental Matrix
Weather alters the physics of a baseball. Hot air makes the ball fly farther; cold air dense-packs the atmosphere; wind blowing out can turn routine flyouts into home runs. This panel monitors these environmental anomalies.

*   **STATUS:** Color-coded safety status:
    *   `☀️ CLEAR` (Safe, open-air stadium).
    *   `🏟️ DOME` (Controlled indoor climate, zero weather impact).
    *   `⛅ DELAY RISK` (Possible rain delay, high danger for starting pitchers who might get warm and then get pulled).
    *   `🌧️ POSTPONE HAZARD` (Severe weather risk. Highly likely to get rained out. Avoid players in this game!).
*   **TEMP & HUMIDITY:** Displays the temperature and moisture. **High heat (85°F+) and high humidity** are massive boosts for hitters, causing the ball to carry significantly farther.
*   **WIND SPEED & DIRECTION:** The velocity and direction of the wind (e.g., `12mph Out to Left`). Wind blowing **OUT** at 8mph+ is a major hitter accelerator. Wind blowing **IN** at 8mph+ is a major pitcher boost.
*   **PARK & UMPIRE:** Evaluates stadium altitude and the historical strike-zone tightness of the assigned home-plate umpire. A "Tight" strike-zone umpire walks more batters, heavily boosting offenses.

## 🚨 Part 2: The Signal Codex

When scanning the dashboards, look for these custom OMEGA badges. They represent immediate, actionable trade and roster signals.

*   `🦈 SHARK (Sharp Money Trigger)`

    *   *How to use it:* Highly reliable core plays. Lock these into your lineups.
*   `🐳 WHALE (Institutional Divergence)`
    *   *What it means:* The biggest high-rollers in the world (the "Whales") are dumping massive financial volume on this play, creating a major gap between ticket count and money handle.
    *   *How to use it:* Elite GPP tournament plays. These indicate massive smart-money backing.
*   `🌪️ STORM (Environmental Accelerator)`
    *   *What it means:* Absolute perfect hitting conditions. High temperatures, high humidity, altitude (like Coors Field), or high wind blowing straight out.
    *   *How to use it:* Prioritize these hitters and stacks. The ball will be flying out of the park.
*   `💨 STEAM (Rapid Money Flow)`
    *   *What it means:* Extreme, late-breaking money is flooding in on this line just hours before lock.
    *   *How to use it:* Pay close attention to these late shifts. They indicate late-breaking sharp news (like a lineup change or bullpen fatigue confirmation).
*   `⚡ BURST (GPP Explosive Pivot)`
    *   *What it means:* A player who has massive statistical upside but is projected for extremely low ownership in GPP tournaments.
    *   *How to use it:* The ultimate tournament secret. Pair a `⚡ BURST` player with a popular stack to make your lineup unique.

### 🔴 The Red Lights (Sell/Fade Signals)

*   `🚨 TRAP (Public Chalk Alert)`
    *   *What it means:* A highly popular "chalk" play that the general public is going to roster heavily, but which has severe underlying warning signs (such as bad prop pricing, low strikeout efficiency, or restricted pitch counts).
    *   *How to use it:* **FADE.** Let the rest of the field roster this player. When they inevitably fail, a massive percentage of your opponents' lineups will be destroyed.
*   `⚠️ PARADOX (Divergence Shield)`
    *   *What it means:* A contradiction in the data (e.g., an elite starting pitcher facing an elite, highly rated offense). 
    *   *How to use it:* Avoid rostering both sides. Choose one side of the matchup to commit to.
*   `🌋 HAZARD (Extreme Risk)`
    *   *What it means:* High probability of negative outcomes—either severe ballpark winds blowing straight in, or a high likelihood of a game being delayed or postponed due to rain.
    *   *How to use it:* Highly risky. Avoid starting pitchers in these games, as a rain delay will end their night prematurely.
*   `🔥 EXHAUSTED / ♨️ GASSED / ♨️ WEARY (Bullpen Alert)`
    *   *What it means:* The opposing team's bullpen has been heavily overworked over the past 3 days. Their top, high-leverage relief pitchers are completely fatigued.
    *   *How to use it:* A massive **BUY signal for the opposing stack**. Once the starting pitcher leaves the game, the offense will feast on tired, backup relief arms, resulting in late-inning run explosions!

To win first place in a high-stakes GPP tournament, you cannot just play the "best" players. You must play the **smartest** players. Here are three professional strategies to execute using the OMEGA Cockpit:

### Strategy 1: The "Trap-Pivot" Sweep
Scan the Starting Pitchers matrix and locate a highly owned pitcher flagged as a `🚨 TRAP` (like Robbie Ray today). 
1.  **Do not roster that pitcher.**
2.  **Pivot** to a pitcher in the same price range who has high Physics (`PHY`) and zero warning signals.
3.  **Directly stack the opposing hitters** (e.g., stack the Arizona Diamondbacks right-handed batters). 
*   *Why this works:* If the trap pitcher fails, the public lineups crash, while your low-owned stack and pivot pitcher vault you straight into the cash.

### Strategy 2: The Bullpen Exhaustion Exploit
Look for a team stack whose opponent is flagged with `🔥 EXHAUSTED` bullpen fatigue.
1.  Even if the starting pitcher is decent, roster a **4-man or 5-man stack** against them.
2.  *Why this works:* Even if the starter pitches well for 4 or 5 innings, the moment they get tired and exit the game, the opposing offense will face fatigued, low-tier bullpen arms. You will capture massive, late-inning run surges that the public never saw coming.

### Strategy 3: The Whale Moneyline Sync
Cross-reference the Team Stack Matrix with the Pitchers Matrix. Locate a team that has a highly positive **Divergence (e.g., +26% money gap)** and a `🐳 WHALE` signal, but their starting pitcher is flagged as a `🚨 TRAP` (low individual strikeouts/outs).
1.  **Do not play the starting pitcher.**
2.  **Roster the team's offense (stack them).**
3.  *Why this works:* The sharp betting markets expect the team to win a high-scoring game (e.g., 8-5). The starting pitcher will get pulled early due to poor efficiency, but the hitters will explode. You get an elite, high-scoring offense at a fraction of the ownership!
