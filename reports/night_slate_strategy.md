## 4. GPP Lineup Construction Strategy (5-Game Slate)

Small slates (5 games) demand a distinct strategy compared to larger slates because ownership consolidates heavily on the top-tier plays. To win a GPP, you must balance raw projection strength with leverage.

### Step 1: Handling Pitcher Ownership
- **Chris Sale (SP1)** is the clear slate-breaker. He is virtually mandatory in cash and should be owned heavily in GPPs. However, because his ownership will be astronomical (likely 60%+), if he gets shelled, the entire field collapses. 
  - **Strategy**: Lock Sale in as SP1, but differentiate elsewhere. If you play a contrarian SP2 and unique stacks, you can safely absorb Sale's high ownership.
- **Shota Imanaga (SP2)** is the ultimate GPP pivot. His recent inflated ERA (`11.49` L3) will scare off public ownership, but his elite `106.3` Alpha and matchup against Oakland (`OAK` has the highest K% in MLB) gives him slate-winning upside at single-digit ownership.
  - **Pairing Recommendation**: **Chris Sale + Shota Imanaga** (Elite strikeout upside, combining high floor with contrarian ceiling).

### Step 2: Selecting Stacks for Leverage
- **The Chalk Stack**: **Los Angeles Dodgers** and **Chicago Cubs** will be highly popular. 
  - **To play them**: If you stack the Dodgers or Cubs, avoid using the standard 1-2-3-4 hitters. Wrap around to the bottom of the order (e.g. hitters 6-7-8-9) or include low-owned one-offs to block duplicate lineups.
- **The OMEGA Pivot Stack**: **Kansas City Royals** (@ Andrew Morris) or **Pittsburgh Pirates** (@ Kai-Wei Teng).
  - Both opposing pitchers are lower-tier arms, and both offenses are projected for solid runs but will command less than half the ownership of the Dodgers/Cubs. Stacking a 5-man Royals stack with Sale/Imanaga gives you massive leverage if the Dodgers underperform.
