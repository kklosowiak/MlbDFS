## What Was Tested

For each of 2,450 games in the 2025 MLB season, the backtest simulated a
'physics-based stack call' using two gates that mirror the DQI scoring engine:

1. **Team xwOBA >= floor** — offensive quality gate (higher = more selective)
2. **Opposing SP physics <= cap** — pitcher vulnerability gate (lower = more selective)

A call fires when BOTH gates pass simultaneously (AND logic).
Success = team scores 4+ or 5+ actual runs in the game.

> **Note:** Vegas divergence, bullpen fatigue, and market signals were not
> available for 2025 and are excluded. This tests the physics foundation only.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Physics threshold validation:** Whether the current SP physics cap (40) and
   xwOBA floor (.300) are the right DQI entry gates historically.
2. **Selectivity tradeoff:** Tighter gates (higher xwOBA, lower SP cap) = fewer
   calls but higher hit rate. Looser gates = more volume but diluted accuracy.
3. **Baseball variance reality:** Even the best physics combo won't exceed ~60%
   because offense is inherently probabilistic — DQI is a filter, not a crystal ball.
