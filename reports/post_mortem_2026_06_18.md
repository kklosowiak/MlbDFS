## 🚀 Tactical Recommendations for Future Slates

When you are playing a small slate (4-5 games) and struggling to distinguish the plays, apply these three core game-theory rules:

### Rule 1: Lean Into Volatility when Pitching is Weak
When every pitcher is rated under 75 and has high volatility (like today), **do not pay up for expensive, vulnerable arms**. Pay down for cheap SPs (like Gage Jump at $7.8K) or bulk relievers (like Sean Burke) to secure a high baseline of points and free up salary for premium bats.

### Rule 2: Coordinate the Pitcher-Stack Correlation (The Upset Script)
If you play a highly contrarian cheap pitcher (like Burke), **couple him with his own team's hitters (White Sox)**. If the pitcher succeeds, it is highly correlated with his team winning and scoring runs. This maximizes your GPP tournament upside.

### Rule 3: Do Not Ignore Raw Mismatch Projections on Small Slates
Even if a team is flagged as a public "Chalk Trap" (like the Royals), if their opponent starting pitcher has a terrible underlying profile (like Matthew Liberatore's 47.8 blended rating), the raw mismatch cannot be ignored on tiny slates where leverage paths are limited. Always hedge a portion of your exposures into these high-implied matchups.
