1. The Physics vs. Liquidity War

Every single night, the MLB arena plays host to a quiet financial war. On one side are millions of retail DFS players and casual bettors, building their rosters based on name recognition, yesterday's box scores, and surface-level team records. On the other side is a network of secretive, quantitative betting syndicates moving millions of dollars on line movements and arbitrage margins.

OMEGA is your personal intelligence terminal in this war. We do not guess, and we do not chase headlines. Instead, OMEGA acts as a high-fidelity probability filter. By constantly monitoring global betting market liquidity (line moves, money-vs-ticket gaps) and cross-referencing it against the immutable physics of the diamond (Statcast exit velocities, air density, pitcher strikeout momentum, and bullpen exhaustion), OMEGA exposes the exact gaps where the public is walking into a trap and the sharps are preparing to cash in.

Whether you are an absolute beginner entering a $1 contest or an advanced DFS pro building 150 lineups for a major tournament, this handbook translates raw terminal signals into a systematic, mathematical edge over the field.

2. Pitchers: Core & Pivots

Rostering starting pitching is about anchoring your slate. In DFS, you need a balance of safety (floor) and strikeout upside (ceiling). OMEGA grades pitchers on a scale from 0 to 110+:

  • OMEGA Score ($\ge 100$ is Elite, $\ge 85$ is Strong): The absolute baseline projection of a pitcher's slate quality. It incorporates vegas win probability, strikeout rates, and runs allowed projection.
  • PHY (Physics Score, 0-40 Scale): Represents the pitcher's raw physical stuff. It measures recent strikeout rate (K/9), swing-and-miss ability (CSW%), and expected ERA (SIERA). **If a pitcher has a high PHY but a low salary, they are a premier value.**
  • MKT (Market Score, 0-20 Scale): The betting market's confidence in this pitcher. It tracks line movement, odds shifts, and sharp betting volume. A **high Market score** means the smartest money in the world is heavily backing this pitcher.
  • Sharp Money Filtering (Shark & Whale): We de-weight starting pitcher sharp multipliers to prioritize individual pitching talent (like strikeouts) over general team moneyline swings. Look for pitchers who combine a high **PHY** score with solid market steam.

3. Hitters: The Smash Vector

Evaluating individual batters requires pairing recent plate appearance momentum with dynamic pitcher matchups.

  • Matchup xwOBA: Regressed dynamically using Bayesian principles to ensure that hitters facing weak pitchers get a proportional boost, while adjusting for small seasonal samples.
  • NPAS Platoon Advantage: The Net Platoon Advantage Score adjusts hitter and pitcher platoon splits. Look for the **ELITE PLATOON** label on player modals, signaling that the hitter's split perfectly exploits the pitcher's weakest splits.
  • 💥 SMASH Factor (Verified 23.7% Hit Rate): To prevent chasing misleading "hot streaks," a hitter only gets flagged with a Smash badge if they have both **positive rolling momentum** (OPS above baseline) and are in an **elite matchup** (high xwOBA or facing a highly vulnerable pitcher). Roster these hitters to target home runs.

4. Stacks & Bullpen Fatigue

Stacking (rostering 3 to 5 players from the same offense) is the single most effective way to win a major DFS tournament because baseball runs are highly correlated.

  • ITT (Implied Team Total): The Vegas projected run total. Stacking teams with an ITT above **4.8** represents the chalk (popular) path to runs, but can be highly owned.
  • Divergence (The Money Gap): The difference between the percentage of money bet on a team vs. the percentage of tickets (public bets) written. If a team has a **+20% Divergence**, it means sharp syndicates are putting massive capital behind this offense.
  • Granular Bullpen Talent Index: Bullpens are graded into 5 tiers (Elite, Strong, Average, Below Average, Weak) with custom multipliers and fatigue rates. Elite bullpens (like Cleveland or Milwaukee) absorb fatigue cleanly, whereas Weak bullpens (like Colorado) crumble rapidly under heavy pitch counts.
  • 🔥 GASSED Bullpen (GPP Attack): Triggered when a weak or fatigued bullpen ($\ge 65$ fatigue) sits behind a short-leash starting pitcher (projected outs $\le 15.5$). This adds a direct **+5.0 Stack Score premium** because the offense is guaranteed multiple high-leverage late innings against tired relief arms.

5. Micro-Environments & Officiating

Weather dictate pitch physics. Cold air is dense and kills fly balls; hot air allows balls to carry. OMEGA reads local weather feeds dynamically and applies environmental modifiers (excluding dome/indoor stadium models):

  • Thermal Density: Outdoor temperatures $\ge 80^\circ$F add a baseline boost to hitter confidence scores and penalize pitchers. Temperatures $< 60^\circ$F do the opposite, dense-packing the air to favor pitchers.
  • Wind Velocity Vectors: Wind blowing OUT to center $\ge 10$ MPH adds a significant boost to stack scores and hitter power ratings. Wind blowing IN at similar speeds acts as a heavy dampener.
  • Umpire Strike Zones: OMEGA references the officiating umpire's historic strike zone. A hitter-friendly umpire (tight strike zone) walks more batters and forces pitchers to throw down the middle, heavily boosting stack upside.

7. The GPP Exploiter Playbook

To win first place in a high-stakes GPP tournament, you cannot just play the popular plays. You must play the **smartest** plays. Here are three step-by-step strategies to win:

Strategy 1: The Leverage Fade Pivot

1. Scan the Team Stack Matrix and locate teams with high implied run totals ($5.0+$) flagged as a `⚠️ FADE RISK`.
2. Do not stack this team. Pivot completely away.
3. Stack the opposing team's offense or pivot to a high-physics stack in the same price range with a positive `Divergence` or `🛸 SNEAKY` badge. If the popular team fails, you vault past a massive portion of the field.

Strategy 2: The Gassed Bullpen Exploit

1. Look for offenses flagged with the `🔥 GASSED BP` badge on the dashboard.
2. Even if the starting pitcher is decent, roster a 4-man or 5-man stack from this team.
3. When the starter exits the game, the offense will feast on tired, lower-tier relief arms, scoring rapid, late-inning runs that generate massive tournament separation.

Strategy 3: The Sneary Stack contrarian Hunt

1. Locate low-owned teams flagged as `🛸 SNEAKY` with low implied totals.
2. Check their matchup details to verify if they are facing a debuting starter, a bullpen opener, or an exhausted bullpen behind a short leash.
3. Stack these hitters. Because the public ignores them due to low Vegas lines, their ownership is near-zero. If their physics override fires, your lineup secures a massive contrarian leverage advantage.
